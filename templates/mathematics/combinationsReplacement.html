Procedure to find Combinations with Replacement

Let us consider Combinations in which repetitions are allowed. That means each time you take an element from the set of n distinct objects you put it back to the set. You might feel the concept of Combinations with Replacement a bit complicated. Luckily, don't worry as we have listed the formula below.

CR(n, r) or C'(n,r) = (r+n-1)!/(r! * (n-1)!)

Where C'(n,r)) is the number of combinations with repetition or replacement

n is the total number of elements in the set,

and r is the number of elements you choose from this set.

Plugin the values of n, r in the corresponding formula, and simplify the equation further to get the number of Combinations with Replacement.

Example: Consider there are five flavors of ice cream butterscotch, vanilla, strawberry, chocolate, banana. You can have three scoops. How many variations are possible and we can repeat?

Solution:

From the given data n = 5

r =3

We know the formula for Combination with Replacement is C'(n,r) = (r+n-1)!/(r! * (n-1)!)

= (3+5-1)!/(3!*(5-1)!)

=(7)!/(3!*(4)!)

= 35

Therefore there are 35 ways of having three scoops from five different flavors.

FAQs on Combinations Replacement Calculator

1. What is the formula for Combinations with Repetition?

The formula for Combinations with Repetition is (r+n-1)!/(r! * (n-1)!)

2. What is meant by Combination with Replacement or Repetition?

The number of ways to choose a sample of r elements from a set of n distinct objects where order does not matter and replacements are allowed.
